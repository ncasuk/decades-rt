Navigator's Display Unit

Introduction

The Navigator's Display Unit previously flown on the C-130 is not available on the 146. However the same function is now provided by HORACE for use on HORACE terminals. This document describes the operation of the NDU function on HORACE. THE INFORMATION DISPLAYED ON THE NAVIGATOR'S DISPLAY UNIT IS INTENDED ONLY TO MEET THE SCIENTIFIC REQUIREMENTS OF A MISSION.

Description

The Navigator's Display Unit (NDU) is provided to aid the execution of scientific missions of the FAAM 146. Its main functions are to provide a display of INS and GPS positions measured by MRF scientific equipment, and to store air or ground positions and display the range and bearing to these positions. The Flight Manager can enter fixed points for the display.

Display format

The full NDU display format is shown below. Note that the bottom two lines are not displayed until air, ground or fixed positions are loaded. If any data are unavailable or known by HORACE to be invalid, then blanks are shown for the relevant numbers. If the display is held as a result of a FREEZE command then all the keywords flash once a second.

                   GPS 51°21.3N 001°27.4W WD 123.4 WS 024.2
                   INe 51°21.3N 001°27.4W MH 123.4 TH 128.2
                   cP1 51°21.3N 001°27.4W MB 123.4 RG 128.2
                   cP2 51°21.3N 001°27.4W MB 123.4 RG 128.2

Key:

GPS

GPS position from our XR5M GPS receiver

WD

Wind direction (0-360° True) from HORACE parameter 571

WS

Wind speed (kts) from HORACE parameter 570

INe

INS or IN2 (corrected) position from our H-423 INS

MH

Magnetic heading (0-360°) from the INS

TH

True heading (0-360°) from the INS

c

A for Air position, G for Ground position, F for Fixed position

APn

Air Position 1 or 2

GPn

Ground Position 1 or 2

FPn

Fixed Position 0 to 9

MB

Magnetic bearing (0-360°) from current INS posn. to Air, Ground or Fixed (True bearing (TB) is used for GPS data)

RG

Range (nmi) from current INS posn to Air, Ground or Fixed Position

The bottom two lines can also take the form:

                   INe AT nnn.n OT snn.nn DA pnn.n GS nnn.n

Where:

INe

INS or IN2 (corrected) position from our H-423 INS

AT

Along track distance (nmi)

OT

Off track distance (nmi), +ve to right of track

DA

Drift angle Port/Stbd (deg)

GS

Ground speed (kts)

Notes:

  1. Winds are only computed every three seconds.
  2. Wind accuracy will be reduced or unreliable if the wind vanes are not fitted.
  3. Wind accuracy is reduced during turns.
  4. GPS accuracy is about 0.1 nmi for 95% of the time, but there is no indication when the accuracy is less than this.
  5. INS accuracy will depend on accuracy of the initial position, together with drift and Schuler errors.
  6. Bearings and ranges are by Great Circle.
  7. Steering to fixed positions is only done within the accuracy of the INS.
  8. If no INS information is available, GPS is used for ranges, bearings, and along and off track distances. In this case the bearings are given as true bearings (TB).

Display options

Eight commands can be given to the NDU by pressing the relevant keys; note that keys 0 and 9 have no effect.

0.

No effect.
   

1.

Stores the present INS position as Air Position 1 (AP1), after which it is displayed on line three of the display. The air position is then allowed to drift with the wind at the time the position was loaded. The bearing and range to the air position are updated once a second.
   

2.

Stores the present INS position as Air Position 2 (AP2), after which it is displayed on line four of the display. The air position is then allowed to drift with the wind at the time the position was loaded. The bearing and range to the air position are updated once a second.
   

3.

Stores the present INS position as the Ground Position 1 (GP1), after which it is displayed on line three of the display, and the bearing and range to the position are updated once a second.
   

4.

Stores the present INS position as the Ground Position 2 (GP2), after which it is displayed on line four of the display, and the bearing and range to the position are updated once a second.
   

5.

Freezes all values on the display and blinks the display keywords. Values continue to updated internally. Press again to resume display update.
   

6.

Steps through any Fixed, AP1 or GP1 positions on line 3 of the display. The final item in the sequence is a line containing Groundspeed, Drift, and the along and off track distances of the current position from the track between the fixed position, if any, displayed on line 4, and the previous fixed position in the fixed position list.
   

7.

Steps through any Fixed, AP2 or GP2 positions on line 4 of the display. The final item in the sequence is a line containing Groundspeed, Drift, and the along and off track distances of the current position from the track between the fixed position, if any, displayed on line 3, and the previous fixed position in the fixed position list.
   

8.

If pressed twice in quick succession, stores the current position difference between the GPS and the INS; also sets display to use IN2 data. If pressed once toggles between using corrected (IN2) and uncorrected (INS) positions for range and bearing to Fixed (not Air or Ground) positions.
   

9.

No effect.

Fixed Positions

As well as using air and ground positions, the NDU can also display the range and bearing to pre-defined fixed positions. These must be entered in the H_NDU.DAT file before or during a flight, using option 12 of the System menu. Any number of positions between 0 and 50 may be entered in the file, and will be used as soon as the file is closed. The first two fixed positions, if any, are displayed on lines 3 and 4 of the NDU when it starts up, and all available air, ground, and fixed positions can be stepped through using option 6 for line 3 and option 7 for line 4. Note that since the NDU uses the INS position to compute range and bearing, it will only steer within the current INS accuracy to any fixed position (typically 1km). It is up to the Flight Manager to enter any required fixed positions. When there is more than one fixed position in the H_NDU.DAT file, the NDU can display the current distance from the track between two adjacent positions.

Possible problems

Display not updating - The H_NDU process on HORACE has stopped or HORACE is not recording data. Restart H_NDU with H_STOP H_NDU and H_START H_NDU.

The GPS display is blank - Blanks are displayed if GPS data is not being received from HORACE every second or the GPS Figure of Merit (FOM) is not zero.

The INS display is blank - Blanks are displayed if the DRS or INS are off, or if the INS is not in GC ALIGN or NAVIGATE mode.

Data problems - Any problems with the accuracy of the information displayed may be due to problems with the original data on HORACE, or with the H_NDU processing program. Note that the winds calculated by HORACE may not agree with 'Navs Winds' since HORACE uses the full gust equations whereas the Navs normally use drift angles and air/ground speed differences.

Unwanted or incorrect fixed positions - H_NDU always uses the fixed positions defined in the file H_NDU.DAT. Unless these have been changed specially for a flight they will be those last used. H_NDU.DAT can be changed at any time, using option 12 of the System menu, and all positions can be commented out if none are required.
